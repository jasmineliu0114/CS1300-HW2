Accessible Components: Radio Buttons

This project explores the design of radio buttons and how changes in the design make trade-offs between learnability, memorability, efficiency, and accessibility for mouse, keyboard, touchscreen, and screen reader users.

Inputs

In the following table, we explore and compare inputs from mouse, keyboard, and touchscreen users for three applications: Google Forms, Amazon Checkout, and Outlook Settings. This includes a focus on what users can and cannot do, functionalities that are learnable, memorable, and efficient, and comparisons between the three applications on interaction and accessibility.

Outputs

Like the section above, we also explore and compare the outputs of the same three applications. In addition to mouse, keyboard, and touchscreen users, there is an additional focus on user who may be using screen readers as well.
